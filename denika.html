How she'll read you

She is a Host

Denika runs Stand Unshaken — courses, memberships, live classes. She knows the loneliness of building a community-powered business firsthand. Speak about Hosts the way you'd speak about her. Treat them as full operators, not "users."

Coach-to-coach

She's a certified life coach who teaches wellness entrepreneurs how to package and price their offers. You speak the same dialect. Talk about product suites, client transformation, daily rhythms — not just "engagement" or "activation."

Faith and integrity

Her brand is explicitly Christian, holistic, alignment-oriented. She's not looking for a sermon — she's looking for someone whose values show up in how they describe the work. Lead with care for the human on the other end. Don't be transactional.

Sales literacy is a plus

140% SDR attainment is not a CSM stat — it's a sales stat. She'll respect commercial fluency. You can talk about renewal conversations, expansion, knowing when to ask for the upgrade. That's a strength most CSMs underplay; don't.

“Help women learn how to create daily rhythms of wellness so they can deeply connect with themselves and their calling.”

— Denika's mission, paraphrased from Endless Sunday. The pattern: daily rhythms, alignment, calling. Mirror that cadence when you talk about your coaching practice.

The story to bring her

For Denika, lead with a story where you held the line on a client's identity, not just their goal. The one where you helped someone choose the role that fit who they were becoming, not the highest-paying one on the table. That story signals (1) you understand alignment over hustle, (2) you can sit with someone in a hard decision, (3) you'd treat her Hosts the way she treats her own community. End with: "That's the work I want to do at scale, alongside Hosts who are already doing it."

Bridge note — if it comes up naturally: Denika and you are both solopreneurs running multi-revenue-stream coaching businesses. Don't force the parallel; let her find it. If she does, she'll feel met.

Common ground hooks (use sparingly, only if natural)

The free tier as conviction

Stand Unshaken's membership is completely free because Denika believes wellness shouldn't be paywalled. ClearCareer ships free job-search tools and free clarity audits for the same reason. If "lead magnet" gets close to the conversation, frame yours as conviction, not funnel hygiene.

You both build the website

Denika designs websites and brand guides for entrepreneurs. You build full marketing sites and AI-powered tools for ClearCareer. When you describe helping a Mighty Host package and launch, lean operational — she'll trust it because she's done it.

Sales-trained CS is a strength, not a flaw

She came from SDR, hit 140% attainment, and led training for the team. You've sold and closed your own coaching offers. CS leaders sometimes hide their sales chops; both of you should not. Talk about expansion, renewal conversations, asking for the upgrade.

Membership-as-product

She runs a membership (Stand Unshaken) inside the very category she now supports at Mighty. You run a coaching program (JSIS) that maps onto the same Host archetype. When you describe Hosts' jobs-to-be-done, you're describing both of you.

The Peer Round

Denika round — questions, intro, what to ask.

After Mary, this round is a decompression. Different mode entirely. Loosen up. The peer round is about whether she'd want to share a Slack channel with you, not whether you can hit a number.

Likely questions from Denika

Walk me through
Can you walk me through your background — what's the short version?
Use the 60-second intro below, not the Mary 90-second one. Lighter. End on community/Host work, not commercials.
Day-to-day
What does a great working relationship with a CSM teammate look like to you? When have you been at your best collaborating?
Tell a story where someone else got the credit. Lighthouse, Dutchie team save, ClearCareer collaborator.
Difficult Host
Tell me about a time you had to work with a difficult Host or client. What did you do?
She's a Host herself. Don't talk about clients like they're problems. Talk about reading the human first, the situation second.
Conflict
How do you handle disagreement with a teammate or manager — especially when you think they're wrong?
She wants to know you can have the hard conversation without making the room weird. Specific story; one minute max.
Ramping
If you joined the team next month, how would you ramp? What do you want from the first 30 days?
Show you'd come in curious, not prescriptive. Ask before you advise. Mirror her own posture.
Why CSM
Why CS? What makes a great CSM, in your view?
Coach-to-coach answer: someone who can hold the Host's outcome as more important than their own ego. Show, don't tell — pick a moment from your own work.

60-second intro for Denika

I've been in the community-coaching-tech intersection for ten-plus years. Most recently building ClearCareer — coached over 200 people through career transitions, ran two top-5% Skool communities, ran live events for 650+ people a month at Lighthouse Labs in a past life.

The throughline is the same thing you do at Stand Unshaken — helping people figure out what they actually want, then giving them the tools to go get it. I've been a Host. I know what it costs to show up for your community every week, and I know how much a good CSM matters when you're trying to build something real.

That's why this seat. I want to do the work I love alongside Hosts who are already doing it.

Questions to ask Denika

Real day
What's a typical day actually look like for you on the CS team? Where do you spend most of your time?
Practical. Reveals the real work, not the job-description version.
Surprise
What's something about working at Mighty that surprised you in a good way? In a hard way?
She'll be candid because she's a peer, not a hiring manager. Listen carefully to the “hard” part.
First 90 days
What tripped you up when you were ramping? What do you wish you'd known sooner?
Asks her to be helpful. Builds rapport. You'll get better intel here than from any executive.
Host-CSM dynamic
You're a Host yourself with Stand Unshaken. How does being a Host change the way you do CSM work for other Hosts?
Direct invitation to talk about what makes her unique. Listen to whether she connects it back to you — that's the moment of mutual recognition.
Team culture
If you described the CSM team's culture in three words to a friend — what are they? And which of those would you want more of?
Forces specificity. Reveals where the team is healthy and where it's not.

Tone reminder: Denika's brand is alignment, daily rhythms, calling. If “why this work” comes up, your answer should sound like it comes from the same family of language. Don't perform it — just don't talk like a sales rep.
